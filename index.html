🔬 Scenario: Retort Processing of Rendang

To guarantee commercial sterility and food safety in ambient Malaysian storage conditions, traditional *Rendang Daging* undergoes thermal retort processing. We model the logarithmic thermal destruction kinetics of Clostridium botulinum spores at a constant processing temperature of $121.1^\circ\text{C}$.

Data References & Frameworks: • MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) Retort Guidelines
• FDA Bad Bug Book (Thermal Inactivation Kinetics: Logarithmic Thermal Death Model)

📐 Bio-Kinetic Functions

Spore Population Curve: f(t)
f(t) = N₀ · e^(-k · t)

Where $N_0 = 1,000,000$ (Initial spore count), and $k = 0.35\text{ min}^{-1}$ (Thermal death kinetic constant derived from standard $D$-value of 6.57 seconds).

Inactivation Rate (Derivative): f'(t)
f'(t) = -k · N₀ · e^(-k · t)

By the Chain Rule, the derivative reveals the instantaneous spore destruction rate per minute at exactly time $t$.
